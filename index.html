🏛️ Founding of Curitiba
History | 1693 | First Settlers

📜 How Curitiba Was Founded

In 1693, a small village was officially recognized in the highlands of what we now call Paraná, Brazil. The name Curitiba comes from the Tupi words "kurytiba", meaning "land of many pinheiros (araucária trees)".


The first inhabitants were Indigenous Tupi-Guarani peoples, Portuguese settlers, and mixed-heritage families called mamelucos. They came searching for gold and built a small community around the Largo da Matriz (today's Tiradentes Square).

👥 Who Were the First People?

  • 🌿 Tupi-Guarani – Indigenous peoples of the land
  • ⛵ Bandeirantes – Portuguese explorers from São Paulo
  • 🏠 Mamelucos – Mixed Portuguese & Indigenous families
  • ⛪ Priests & Missionaries – Built first churches

🏙️ Important First Places

  • ⛪ Largo da Matriz – First public square
  • 🏛️ Casa da Câmara – First town hall (1668)
  • 🌲 Araucária Forests – Symbol of Curitiba
  • 🏔️ Serra do Mar – Mountain barrier to coast

📖 Historical Chronicle
Portuguese Reading | Leitura de Crônica Histórica

📝 A Crônica: "O Dia em que Curitiba Nasceu"

Era uma manhã de neblina quando os primeiros moradores subiram o caminho de terra entre as araucárias gigantes. O ano era 1693, e aquele alto planalto seria, um dia, uma das cidades mais belas do Brasil.

Os índios Tupi-Guarani já conheciam aquelas terras. Chamavam o lugar de kurytiba – a terra dos pinheiros. Os bandeirantes portugueses chegaram mais tarde, com seus cavalos cansados e sonhos de ouro.

Juntos, eles construíram uma pequena vila ao redor de uma praça. Ergueram uma igrejinha branca, uma casa da câmara, e plantaram seus primeiros jardins. As crianças brincavam entre as raízes das araucárias enquanto os adultos trabalhavam.

"Esta terra é boa," disse um velho bandeirante, olhando para o horizonte verde. "Aqui vamos ficar."

E ficaram. E Curitiba cresceu.

🕰️ Curitiba Historical Timeline

1500 – Portuguese Arrival in Brazil

Pedro Álvares Cabral arrives on the coast of Brazil. The journey to the interior begins!

1610 – First Expeditions to Paraná

Bandeirantes explore the highland plateau. They discover the land of pinheiros.

1668 – First Town Hall Built

The Casa da Câmara is constructed. This is the first government building!

🌟 1693 – Official Founding of Curitiba

Curitiba is officially recognized as a Vila (village). Happy Birthday, Curitiba!

1721 – Tropeiros Arrive

Cattle drovers pass through Curitiba on the famous Caminho do Viamão.

1853 – Capital of Paraná

Curitiba becomes the capital of the new Province of Paraná. The city grows fast!

🐎 Tropeiros & Historic Center
The Cattle Drivers Who Built Downtown Curitiba

🐄 Who Were the Tropeiros?

The Tropeiros were cattle drivers who traveled long distances through South America, moving herds of cattle and mules from Rio Grande do Sul to São Paulo.


They passed through Curitiba on the famous Caminho do Viamão. Their stopping point became a trading post — the origin of Curitiba's historic center!

🏘️ How the City Was Shaped

As more tropeiros arrived, markets, inns, and churches were built around the main square. The Largo da Matriz became the heart of the city.


Today you can still visit this historic area! The Rua das Flores and Largo da Ordem are part of this original neighborhood.

🗺️ The Tropeiros' Route

🐄 Rio Grande do Sul  →  📍 Curitiba  →  Sorocaba  →  São Paulo
Caminho do Viamão – Over 3,000 km traveled on foot and horseback!

🏔️ The Relief of Curitiba's Region

🌊 Rivers of Curitiba

  • 💧 Rio Iguaçu – The largest river of Paraná
  • 💧 Rio Barigui – Flows through parks in the west
  • 💧 Rio Belém – Crosses the city center
  • 💧 Rio Atuba – Northern border of Curitiba
  • 💧 Rio Passaúna – Water source in the west

🏔️ Types of Relief

  • ⛰️ Serra do Mar – Coastal mountain range
  • 🌄 Planalto Curitibano – The highland plateau
  • 🌾 Vale do Rio Iguaçu – River valley
  • 🏞️ Parques – Parks in old riverbeds
  • 🌲 Araucária Forest – Native highland forest
